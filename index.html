How it works

Simple for customers,
hands-off for your team

1

Tap to rent

Customers tap a contactless card, Apple Pay, or Google Pay — or scan a QR code — to unlock a power bank. No app needed for card and wallet payments.

2

Take it anywhere

The power bank goes with them. They charge their device wherever they are in the venue — at their table, at the bar, wherever suits.

3

Return when done

When they're finished, they return the power bank to any available slot on the same station. Simple, self-contained, and no staff needed.

Revenue share

We place the unit and handle pricing centrally. Your venue earns a share of every rental with no outlay and no overhead. We look after the hardware.

From the founder

I kept noticing people leave pubs and cafés early because their phone was about to die.

It's not dramatic — they just start making excuses to go. Power bank rental has been common overseas for a few years now, and it felt like the right time to bring a practical version of it to New Zealand venues.

WattMate is an early-stage rollout. We're currently looking for pilot venues in Auckland — places willing to try it with low commitment and see what the data looks like. The whole arrangement is designed to be easy to try and easy to exit if it's not working.

If you run a venue and the idea sounds interesting, I'd genuinely like to hear from you. No hard sell — just a conversation to see if it makes sense for your space.
